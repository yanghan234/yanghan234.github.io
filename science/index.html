      During the past 5 years, I have been working on methodology development in computational chemistry.

      Accelerating G0W0 Calculations

      We propose an approximation of density-density response functions of molecules and solids that leads to an improvement of the efficiency of G0W0 calculations, without any substantial reduction in accuracy. The approximation is based on the calculations of a subset of eigenvectors of the dielectric matrix using the kinetic operator instead of the full Hamiltonian. The computational savings introduced by this approximation depend on the system, and they become more substantial as the number of electrons increases.

      Our paper: Improving the efficiency of G0W0 calculations with approximate spectral decompositions of dielectric matrices. Han Yang, Marco Govoni and Giulia Galli, J. Chem. Phys. 151, 224102 (2019), also on arXiv.

      Computing Electron-Phonon Interactions in Large Scale Systems

      Recently a method was proposed to couple the calculations of G0W0 quasiparticle energies and electron-phonon scattering in molecules and nanostructures by using the spectral decomposition of dielectric matrices. Here we generalize this approach to solids. Our implementation in the WEST code (www.west-code.org) does not require summation over conduction bands or any self-consistent density-functional-perturbation-theory calculations. We validate our framework by computing phonon frequencies and electron-phonon lifetimes of several semiconductors and insulators, and we discuss the accuracy and efficiency of the method relative to existing techniques.

      This is an ongoing project.
