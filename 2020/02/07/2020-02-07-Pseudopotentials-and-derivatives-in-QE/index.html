    Pseudopotentials and their derivatives in Quantum Espresso
    2020/02/07 Research QE PseudoPotential

    In this article, I’m going to summarize technical details about pseudopotentials as in Quantum Espresso ( QE ), especially the derivatives of pseudopotentials because they are important components involved in phonon calculation. Equations are valid in all version; while the codes might not be valid for all version of QE. In this article, the codes are written especially for QE v6.1.

    General idea of pseudopotentials

    Pseudopotentials (PP), also known as effective potentials in quantum chemistry community, are the functions the describe the potentials of core electrons in quantum physics community. People prefer to use pseudopotentials for two reasons. 1) Core electrons usually do not contribute to chemical processes like forming chemical bonds; 2) Due to the singularity of Coulomb interaction at nucleus, it is not easy to use numerical approximate basis to describe the singularity.

    Quantum chemistry communities use several gaussian functions at core to approximate this function, while in quantum physics community, where plane wave basis is popular, it is very difficult to deal with problem without pseudopotentials. For more detailed descriptions of pseudopotentials, check out the wiki page Pseudopotential.

    Categories of pseudopotentials

    Although other kinds of pseudopotentials do exist, the following three flavors are most widely accepted, norm conserving, ultrasoft and projector augmented wave(PAW). Ultrasoft and PAW usually require lower plane wave cutoff compared to norm-conserving case, but they require more effort in terms of implementation. This article focuses on norm-conserving pseudopotentials.
